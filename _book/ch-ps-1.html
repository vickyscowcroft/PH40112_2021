Relativistic Cosmology Part 2

Problems sheet 1

Q1. Geometry of the Universe

The Robertson-Walker metric is defined as \[\begin{equation} ds^2 = -c^2 dt^2 + a^2(t) \left[\dfrac{dr^2}{1 - kr^2} + r^2\left(d\theta^2 + \sin^2\theta d\phi^2\right)\right]. \end{equation}\]

  1. What are the possible values of \(k\) and what to they correspond to?
  2. Find the distance, \(S\), between the origin (\(r=0\)) and an object at \(r = r_0\) at a fixed time \(t_0\) for each of the three values of \(k\).

Q2. Evolution of the Universe

In this question you will need the Friedman equation:

\[\begin{equation} \left(\dfrac{\dot{a}}{a}\right)^2 = \dfrac{8\pi G}{3}\rho - \dfrac{kc^2}{a^2} \end{equation}\]

and the Fluid equation:

\[\begin{equation} \dot{\rho} + 3\dfrac{\dot{a}}{a}\left(\rho + \dfrac{P}{c^2}\right) = 0 \end{equation}\]

The equations of state for matter and radiation are:

\[\begin{align} \begin{array}{lr} P_{\text{mat}} = 0, & P_{\text{rad}} = \dfrac{\rho c^2}{3}\\ \end{array} \end{align}\]

Derive expressions for

  1. \(\rho(a)\)
  2. \(a(t)\)
  3. \(\rho(t)\)

assuming a flat, matter dominated universe and radiation dominated universe.

Q3. Equations of state

We will now assume that the energy density of the Universe is dominated by a fluid with the equation of state \[\begin{equation} P = (\gamma - 1)\rho c^2 \end{equation}\]

where \(\gamma\) is a constant in the range \(0 < \gamma < 2\).

Derive expressions for

  1. \(\rho(a)\)
  2. \(a(t)\)
  3. \(\rho(t)\)

again assuming the Universe is flat.

Q4. Strange fluids

  1. Use your answer from Q3 to show how \(\rho(a)\), \(a(t)\) and \(\rho(t)\) evolve if \(\gamma = -1\).
  2. What value of \(\gamma\) is required for \(\rho\) to have the same time dependance as the \(k/a^2\) term in the Friedman equation?
  3. Assuming \(k<0\), find the solution \(a(t)\) for this type of fluid.
